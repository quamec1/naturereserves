Bunso Aboretum Forest Reserve

Bunso Aboretum Forest Reserve is located in the Eastern Region of Ghana, south of the town of Bunso. It was founded in 1935 by two British men namely Frank Thompson and David Gillet but later developed into a recreational park by Ghanaians in collaboration with the Ministry of Tourism Ghana. The site has over 600 species of trees used for various medicinal purposes. The Bunso Park is a dense forest endowed with many medicinal plants, herbs and wild animals. The arboretum has within its enclave over 600 species of plants, 105 species of birds, 57 species of snakes, 300 speciesof butterflies, assorted fruits including; grapes, passion and star fruit.
